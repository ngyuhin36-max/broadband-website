Guangzhou Hotel Area Guide

Zhujiang New Town / Tianhe CBD — High-End Business & Skyscrapers

Zhujiang New Town is Guangzhou's financial and business core. Landmarks such as Canton Tower, Guangzhou Opera House, and Guangdong Provincial Museum are all located here. Top international hotels including Four Seasons, W, Rosewood, Park Hyatt, and Ritz-Carlton cluster in this area, with high-floor rooms offering Pearl River and Canton Tower views. Metro Lines 3 and 5 intersect here for very convenient transport — a top choice for business travellers and those seeking skyline views. 5-star hotels from HK$900.

Tianhe District — Shopping & Entertainment Hub

Tianhe District is Guangzhou's shopping and entertainment centre, with major malls including Tee Mall, Grandview Plaza, and TaiKoo Hui. International brand hotels such as Mandarin Oriental, Marriott, and Shangri-La are located here, near Tiyu Xilu and Tianhe South metro stations for very convenient transport and shopping. Well-suited for travellers focused on shopping and entertainment. 5-star hotels from HK$600.

Beijing Road / Yuexiu District — Millennium-Old Commercial & Cultural Heritage

Beijing Road Pedestrian Street is the historic commercial heart of Guangzhou's thousand-year-old trading history, with preserved ancient road surfaces underground. The area features the Museum of the Nanyue King, Chen Clan Ancestral Hall, and other historic sites, with traditional food and snack shops everywhere. Hotel options range from budget to 4-star. Ideal for travellers seeking historic and cultural experiences with local cuisine. 4-star hotels from HK$350.

Shamian / Liwan District — European Architecture & Dim Sum Culture

Shamian Island is one of Guangzhou's most distinctive historic districts, preserving many European-style colonial buildings — a picturesque area for strolling and photography. White Swan Hotel is located here, one of Guangzhou's heritage hotels. Liwan District is the birthplace of dim sum culture, with classic teahouses like Tao Tao Ju and Guangzhou Restaurant. Ideal for travellers wanting to experience authentic Cantonese dim sum culture and European architectural charm. 5-star hotels from HK$800.

Panyu Chimelong — Family Holiday Destination

Chimelong Resort in Panyu District features Chimelong Safari Park, Chimelong Paradise, and Chimelong Water Park among its theme parks. Chimelong Hotel and Panda Hotel are the resort's official hotels, with guests enjoying priority park entry. Ideal for family holidays — we recommend planning a 2-day/1-night or longer itinerary. Chimelong Hotel from HK$900, Panda Hotel from HK$500.

Guangzhou South Station / Panyu — High-Speed Rail Hub

Guangzhou South Station is the direct high-speed rail destination from Hong Kong, reachable in just 47 minutes from Hong Kong West Kowloon Station. Several budget and mid-range hotels surround the station, suitable for late-night arrivals or early-morning departures. Take Metro Line 2 for about 30 minutes to reach the city centre at Zhujiang New Town. Budget hotels near Guangzhou South Station from HK$150.

Accommodation by Traveller Type

Families — Chimelong Safari Park + Panda Hotel

For families with children visiting Guangzhou, Chimelong Resort is the top choice. Chimelong Hotel connects directly to the Safari Park, with guests enjoying priority entry and themed rooms plus a water park. For tighter budgets, Chimelong Panda Hotel features panda-themed rooms that children love, from HK$500/night. We suggest a 2-day/1-night itinerary: Day 1 Chimelong Safari Park, Day 2 Chimelong Paradise or Water Park. For city-based family activities, Grandview Plaza (with Grandview Aquarium) is also recommended — stay at Marriott Hotel Tianhe for walking-distance access.

Dim Sum & Food — Shamian / Liwan / Beijing Road

Guangzhou is the birthplace of dim sum culture. For authentic Cantonese dim sum, Shamian / Liwan District is the top pick. White Swan Hotel's own Yutangchunnuan restaurant is a renowned dim sum venue, and classic teahouses like Tao Tao Ju and Guangzhou Restaurant are within walking distance. Beijing Road / Yuexiu District also offers abundant traditional food — rice noodle rolls, wonton noodles, claypot rice and more. Consider staying at Jianguo Hotel or nearby budget options from HK$350/night to explore old Guangzhou's food culture on foot. Food trail: morning tea (Tao Tao Ju) → lunch (Yinji rice rolls) → afternoon tea (Dian Du De) → dinner (Bingsheng).

Business Travel — Zhujiang New Town / Tianhe

Business travellers prioritise transport and meeting facilities. Zhujiang New Town hosts Four Seasons, Park Hyatt, Rosewood, and Ritz-Carlton, close to major corporate headquarters and the Guangzhou International Convention Centre. Tianhe District features Marriott and Mandarin Oriental in the commercial core with comprehensive business facilities. Most 5-star hotels offer executive lounge services including complimentary afternoon tea and happy hour. During Canton Fair periods (April and October each year), hotel availability is tight — booking 1-2 months ahead is recommended.

Weekend Rail Getaway — 47 Minutes from Hong Kong

From Hong Kong West Kowloon Station, the high-speed train reaches Guangzhou South Station in just 47 minutes — even a same-day return trip is feasible. We suggest departing Saturday morning, then taking Metro Line 2 about 30 minutes to the city centre. Stay one night in Zhujiang New Town or Tianhe District, enjoy morning dim sum the next day, then head back. Book train tickets 7 days in advance via 12306 or Trip.com. Weekend itinerary suggestion: Canton Tower sightseeing → Pearl River night cruise → Day 2 Shamian dim sum → Beijing Road shopping.

Guangzhou Hotel FAQ

Q: Which area in Guangzhou is good for hotels?
Sightseeing travellers should consider Beijing Road / Yuexiu District for its rich millennium-old cultural heritage. Business travellers are well-served by Zhujiang New Town or Tianhe District. For dim sum experiences, Shamian / Liwan District is ideal. Families should look at Panyu Chimelong Resort.
Q: What hotels are recommended in Zhujiang New Town?
Top hotels in Zhujiang New Town include: Four Seasons Hotel (IFC top floors, from HK$1,200), W Hotel (from HK$900), Rosewood (from HK$1,100), Park Hyatt (from HK$1,000), and Ritz-Carlton (from HK$950). All offer Pearl River and Canton Tower views.
Q: Where should I stay near Chimelong?
The top choice is Chimelong Hotel (from HK$900), with direct access to Safari Park and priority entry for guests. For tighter budgets, Chimelong Panda Hotel (from HK$500) offers the same resort guest privileges. Budget hotels are also available nearby.
Q: How much do Guangzhou hotels cost per night?
Guangzhou hotel prices vary widely: budget hotels (7 Days / Home Inn) HK$150-300/night, mid-range 4-star HK$350-600/night, 5-star international brands HK$500-1,200/night. Prices rise 50-100% during Canton Fair periods.
Q: How long is the high-speed train from Hong Kong to Guangzhou?
The high-speed train from Hong Kong West Kowloon Station goes directly to Guangzhou South Station in about 47 minutes. Tickets cost approximately HK$215 (second class). From Guangzhou South, take Metro Line 2 about 30 minutes to the city centre (Gongyuanqian / Haizhu Square stations). Booking 7 days ahead is recommended.
Q: Where are the good dim sum spots in Guangzhou?
Notable dim sum restaurants include: Tao Tao Ju (Liwan flagship / Tianhe branch), Dian Du De (multiple locations), Guangzhou Restaurant (Wenchangnan Road flagship), and White Swan Hotel's Yutangchunnuan. Go between 7-9am to skip the queues — around CNY 50-80 per person (approx. HK$55-88).
Q: What should international visitors note about Guangzhou hotels?
Key reminders: (1) Check-in requires a valid passport or travel document; (2) Some budget hotels may not accept international guests — consider international brand hotels or those marked "accepts international guests" on Trip.com; (3) Download WeChat Pay or Alipay in advance for daily spending; (4) Guangzhou has internet restrictions — a VPN is needed for Google, WhatsApp, etc.
Q: Is Guangzhou metro convenient?
Guangzhou's metro network is extensive, with 16+ lines covering all major areas citywide. Fares are CNY 2-12 (approx. HK$2.2-13), much cheaper than taxis. Staying near a metro station (within 5 minutes walk) is recommended. Line 3 (Guangzhou South Station / Zhujiang New Town / Tianhe) and Line 1 (Ximenkou / Gongyuanqian / Tiyu Xilu) are commonly used by travellers.
Q: How do I get to Canton Tower?
Canton Tower is located south of Zhujiang New Town. Take Metro Line 3 to Canton Tower Station for direct access. Observation deck tickets cost around CNY 150 (approx. HK$165). Arriving 30 minutes before sunset lets you enjoy the full view from dusk to nightfall over the Pearl River. Hotels in Zhujiang New Town (Four Seasons / W / Rosewood) are about 15-20 minutes walk away.
Q: Which Guangzhou hotels have swimming pools?
Hotels with pools include: W Hotel (rooftop pool with Pearl River views), Four Seasons Hotel (heated indoor pool), Ritz-Carlton (indoor pool), and Chimelong Hotel (water park). Pools are very popular during Guangzhou's hot summers.
Q: Where should I stay during the Canton Fair?
The Canton Fair exhibition halls are in Pazhou. Staying in Zhujiang New Town or Tianhe District with direct metro to Pazhou Station is recommended. During Canton Fair periods (half a month each in April and October), hotel availability is tight and prices rise 50-100% — booking 1-2 months ahead is advised. Garden Hotel and Shangri-La are popular among Canton Fair business travellers.
Q: What is hotel breakfast like in Guangzhou?
5-star hotel buffet breakfasts typically offer both Chinese and Western options of high quality, at CNY 100-200/person (approx. HK$110-220). However, given Guangzhou's rich dim sum culture, we recommend skipping hotel breakfast at least one day to experience authentic morning tea at a nearby teahouse — around CNY 50-80 per person for a more memorable meal.
