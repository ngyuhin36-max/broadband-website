Taipei Hotel Area Guide

Xinyi District — Shopping & Entertainment Hub

Xinyi District is Taipei's commercial and entertainment core, home to Taipei 101 and major department stores in the Xinyi shopping area (Shin Kong Mitsukoshi, Breeze Centre, ATT 4 FUN). The area hosts top-rated international hotels including W Taipei, Grand Hyatt, and Le Meridien. Taipei City Hall MRT Station and Taipei 101/World Trade Centre Station provide highly convenient transport. It is the top-rated stay area for shopping and nightlife enthusiasts. Five-star hotels start from HK$1,200 per night, ideal for travellers seeking a vibrant urban experience.

Ximending — Youth & Street Culture Hub

Ximending is Taipei's youth culture epicentre, brimming with street culture, food stalls, cinema streets, and trendy shops. Just Sleep and amba hotel are highly popular accommodation choices in the area. Ximen MRT Station provides excellent connectivity across the city. Hotels here are primarily mid-range, with four-star options from HK$550. It is an excellent base for young travellers and first-time Taipei visitors. Night market food is within walking distance, with highly convenient amenities all around.

Zhongshan District — Arts & Food Paradise

Zhongshan District hosts multiple top-tier hotels including the Regent Taipei, Grand Mayfull Hotel, and Taipei Marriott. Zhongshan North Road is a traditional upscale commercial area, while Chifeng Street and the Zhongshan Station underground mall are arts-lover essentials. The area offers an exceptional variety of restaurants, with Japanese cuisine being particularly outstanding. Zhongshan and Shuanglian MRT Stations provide comprehensive amenities, with five-star hotels from HK$1,100 and four-star from HK$700. Ideal for travellers seeking food and cultural experiences.

Da'an District — Quality Living Choice

Da'an District is Taipei's upscale residential area, encompassing the Eastern District shopping zone, Zhongxiao Fuxing commercial area, and Yongkang Street food quarter. Shangri-La Far Eastern is located on Dunhua South Road, offering convenient transport in a relatively peaceful setting. Yongkang Street's Din Tai Fung and mango shaved ice are must-visit destinations. Zhongxiao Fuxing and Da'an MRT Stations offer excellent amenities, with five-star hotels from HK$1,500. Ideal for travellers who value quality living and fine dining.

Beitou District — Hot Spring Resort Destination

Beitou District is Taipei's suburban hot spring resort area, reachable from the city centre in approximately 30-40 minutes by MRT. Grand View Resort Beitou, Kagaya, and other hot spring hotels offer top-tier soaking experiences. Beitou Hot Spring Museum and Thermal Valley are within walking distance. Hot spring hotels start from HK$800 (including private hot spring bath), ideal for couples and relaxation seekers. Particularly popular during the autumn and winter seasons.

Taipei Main Station / Zhongzheng District — Transport Hub

Taipei Main Station is the city's central transport hub, where MRT, HSR, TRA, and Airport MRT converge. Caesar Park Hotel and Cosmos Hotel are directly opposite the station for exceptional convenience. The station-front underground mall and Huayin Street offer abundant shopping options. Ideal for travellers who need frequent rail access or early morning flights. Four-star hotels from HK$650, three-star from HK$400, offering excellent value for money.

Hotel Recommendations by Traveller Type

Family Travel — Safety & Comfort Are Essential

When travelling to Taipei with children, prioritise hotels with pools and kids' facilities. Grand Hyatt Taipei features an outdoor pool and children's club, conveniently next to Taipei 101. Taipei Marriott Hotel is near the Miramar Ferris Wheel, a favourite with young children. For tighter budgets, Just Sleep or Caesar Park Hotel offer clean, well-maintained rooms at affordable prices. We recommend choosing a breakfast-inclusive package — Taipei hotel buffet breakfasts offer extensive variety, so the whole family can fuel up before a day of exploration. Suggested family itinerary: Taipei 101 + Taipei Zoo + Children's Amusement Park + National Palace Museum. Staying in Xinyi or Zhongshan District is highly convenient.

Couples' Getaway — Romantic Photo-Worthy Stays

For a couples' trip to Taipei, W Taipei is a top choice — Xinyi District night views paired with the WET BAR rooftop pool make for unforgettable photos. For a romantic hot spring experience, Grand View Resort Beitou's private onsen with mountain views creates a wonderful atmosphere. Business Travel — Efficiency & Transport Come First

Business travellers prioritise transport and meeting facilities. In Zhongshan District, the Regent Taipei is at the commercial core, near multiple corporate headquarters. Most five-star hotels provide business centres and complimentary WiFi. When booking, check if executive lounge access is included, which typically offers complimentary afternoon tea and Happy Hour. Caesar Park Hotel at Taipei Main Station offers outstanding transport hub advantages, ideal for HSR business trips.

Backpackers / Budget Travel — Smart Savings Tips

For budget-friendly Taipei hotels, Cosmos Hotel starts from just HK$400 per night, located next to Taipei Main Station with excellent transport links. Booking tips: avoid New Year's Eve (31 December) and Lunar New Year; book 2-3 weeks in advance for early-bird discounts; make use of platform membership discounts. Additionally, March-May and September-November are Taipei's relative off-season, with room rates 20-30% lower than summer and New Year's Eve. Taipei Hotel Accommodation FAQ

Q: Which area in Taipei is ideal for staying?
For shopping and entertainment, Xinyi District is the top choice, right next to Taipei 101 and major department stores. For youth culture, Ximending is recommended. For arts and food, choose Zhongshan District. For quality living and Yongkang Street cuisine, Da'an District. For hot springs, head to Beitou District. For transport convenience, Taipei Main Station area.
Q: What hotels are near Taipei 101?
Hotels within walking distance of Taipei 101 include: Grand Hyatt Taipei (5-minute walk), W Taipei (8-minute walk), and Le Meridien (10-minute walk). Staying in Xinyi District is recommended, especially during New Year's Eve when you can enjoy the spectacular 101 fireworks from your room.
Q: Where are hot spring hotels in Taipei?
Taipei's hot spring hotels are concentrated in Beitou District, reachable by MRT in approximately 30-40 minutes from the city centre. Top-rated options include Grand View Resort Beitou (white sulphur spring, from HK$1,600) and Kagaya (Japanese-style hot spring inn). Most hot spring hotels feature private in-room hot spring baths, plus communal open-air hot spring pools.
Q: How much does a Taipei hotel cost per night?
Taipei hotel price ranges: budget hotels HK$300-500/night, mid-range 4-star HK$550-900/night, five-star international brands HK$1,100-2,800/night. Prices rise 30-50% during New Year's Eve and Lunar New Year. Off-season (March-May, September-November) five-star hotels can be 20-30% less.
Q: Is Taipei MRT transport convenient?
Taipei's MRT system is highly developed, covering all major city areas, with fares of NT$20-65 (approx. HK$5-16). We recommend staying near an MRT station (within a 5-minute walk) to significantly reduce travel time. The Bannan Line (Blue Line) and Tamsui-Xinyi Line (Red Line) are the most commonly used by travellers. Operating hours are approximately 06:00-24:00.
Q: What should travellers know before visiting Taipei?
Important reminders: (1) Check visa/entry permit requirements for your nationality; (2) Taipei uses New Taiwan Dollar (NT$), approximately 1 HK$ = 3.9-4.1 NT$; (3) Most Taipei hotels accept credit cards, but night market stalls require cash; (4) Voltage is 110V — bring a plug adapter; (5) Google, WhatsApp, and other global apps work normally in Taipei — no VPN needed; (6) Consider downloading the EasyCard app or purchasing a physical EasyCard for convenient transport.
Q: What is Taipei hotel breakfast like?
Five-star hotel buffet breakfasts typically offer a blend of Western, Chinese, and Japanese options with excellent quality, at NT$600-1,000 per person (approx. HK$150-250). Grand Hyatt's Cafe and Regent Taipei breakfast experiences are highly recommended. Mid-range hotel breakfasts cost NT$200-400 per person with simpler but adequate selections. For an authentic Taipei breakfast experience, try a local soy milk shop or egg crepe (danbing) stall near your hotel.
Q: Which Taipei hotels have swimming pools?
Taipei hotels with swimming pools include: W Taipei (WET BAR rooftop pool), Grand Hyatt Taipei (outdoor pool), Shangri-La Far Eastern (high-altitude pool), Grand Mayfull Hotel (indoor pool), and Regent Taipei (indoor pool). Outdoor pools are especially popular in summer, and some hotel pools require advance reservation.
Q: How do I get from Hong Kong to Taipei?
The primary way from Hong Kong to Taipei is by direct flight, taking approximately 1 hour 45 minutes, landing at Taoyuan International Airport (TPE). From Taoyuan Airport to central Taipei, take the Airport MRT (approximately 35 minutes to Taipei Main Station, NT$160) or bus (approximately 50-70 minutes, NT$125-145). Staying near Taipei Main Station is recommended for convenient airport transport connections.
Q: Are there hotels near Taipei night markets?
Hotels are available near all major Taipei night markets: Shilin Night Market (near Jiantan MRT Station), Raohe Night Market (near Songshan Station), Ningxia Night Market (near Zhongshan Station, 10-minute walk), and Ximending Night Market (Ximen Station). Staying in Zhongshan District or Ximending is recommended, with multiple night markets within walking distance for maximum convenience.
Q: Where should I stay for Taipei New Year's Eve?
For Taipei's iconic 101 fireworks on New Year's Eve, Xinyi District hotels are the top choice: Grand Hyatt, W Taipei, and Le Meridien all offer rooms with fireworks views or nearby vantage points. New Year's Eve room rates typically rise 50-100%, so book 2-3 months in advance. On a budget, staying in Zhongshan District or Ximending and taking the MRT to Xinyi District is also viable — the MRT operates all night on New Year's Eve.
