Kaohsiung Hotel Area Guide

Pier-2 / Yancheng — Arts & Harbour Culture Hub

Yancheng District is the cultural heart of Kaohsiung, home to the renowned Pier-2 Art District with its warehouses-turned-galleries, street art, and creative markets. The Love River runs through the area, offering romantic evening boat rides. Chateau de Chine and Lees Boutique Resort are popular accommodation choices here. Yancheng MRT Station provides good connectivity, and the area is walkable with numerous cafes and heritage buildings. Hotels start from HK$500 per night, ideal for art lovers and culture seekers.

Formosa Boulevard / Xinxing — Night Market & Transport Hub

Xinxing District is centred around the famous Formosa Boulevard MRT Station, featuring the stunning Dome of Light glass installation. The area is home to Liuhe Night Market, one of Taiwan's most famous night markets, and Kaohsiung Main Station. Just Sleep and Kindness Hotel are highly popular choices here. With MRT Red and Orange lines intersecting at Formosa Boulevard, transport is extremely convenient. Budget hotels from HK$250, mid-range from HK$400, making it ideal for first-time visitors and night market food enthusiasts.

Qianzhen / Asia New Bay Area — Modern Waterfront District

The Asia New Bay Area is Kaohsiung's newest and most dynamic waterfront development, home to the Kaohsiung Exhibition Centre, Kaohsiung Main Public Library, and the Kaohsiung Music Centre. Top luxury hotels including Silks Club, Kaohsiung Marriott, and InterContinental are all located here. The area offers stunning harbour views and modern architecture. Light rail connects the area to Pier-2 and other attractions. Five-star hotels from HK$1,000, ideal for luxury travellers and business visitors.

Sizihwan / Hamasen — Seaside & Sunset Destination

Sizihwan is famous for its stunning sunsets over the Taiwan Strait, consistently ranked among Taiwan's top sunset viewing spots. The nearby Hamasen neighbourhood features Japanese-era heritage buildings, a star tunnel, and a charming fishing harbour. While hotel options are limited in this immediate area, staying in nearby Yancheng or Qianjin provides easy MRT access. The area is ideal for couples and photography enthusiasts. A day trip from central Kaohsiung takes approximately 20 minutes by MRT.

Zuoying HSR Station — High Speed Rail Gateway

Zuoying is where the Taiwan High Speed Rail (HSR) terminal is located, making it the gateway for travellers arriving from Taipei (approximately 1.5 hours by HSR). The nearby Lotus Pond scenic area features the iconic Dragon and Tiger Pagodas, Spring and Autumn Pavilion, and numerous temples. Hotels near Zuoying Station are convenient for early departures or late arrivals. The area also provides access to the Kaohsiung Arena entertainment district. Mid-range hotels from HK$400.

E-Da World / Dashu — Theme Park Resort Area

Located in the hillside area of Dashu District, E-Da World is southern Taiwan's largest theme park and outlet shopping complex. E-Da Royal Hotel is directly connected to the park, offering the most convenient access. The area is approximately 40 minutes from central Kaohsiung by shuttle bus. Ideal for families with children who want a full resort experience. Hotel packages often include theme park tickets, offering good overall value. Five-star resort accommodation from HK$800 per night.

Hotel Recommendations by Traveller Type

Family Travel — E-Da World & Cijin Island Adventures

Kaohsiung is an excellent family destination with plenty of child-friendly attractions. E-Da Royal Hotel is the top family pick, directly connected to E-Da World theme park with rides, an outlet mall, and a water park in summer. For a beach day, take the ferry from Gushan to Cijin Island for seafood, sand, and the historic Cijin Lighthouse. The Kaohsiung Museum of Science and Technology offers interactive exhibits that children love. For accommodation, E-Da Royal Hotel (from HK$800) provides resort-style facilities, while budget-conscious families can choose Just Sleep near Kaohsiung Station (from HK$400) for easy MRT access to all attractions.

Couples' Getaway — Sizihwan Sunsets & Pier-2 Romance

Kaohsiung offers some of Taiwan's most romantic settings for couples. Watch the sunset at Sizihwan Beach, widely regarded as one of Taiwan's top sunset spots, then stroll through the atmospheric Hamasen Star Tunnel. Explore Pier-2 Art District hand-in-hand, browsing galleries and creative shops. For dinner, enjoy harbour-view dining at the Asia New Bay Area. Silks Club (from HK$1,200) offers the most refined romantic stay, while Hotel Indigo (from HK$700) provides boutique charm near Central Park. WO Hotel's rooftop bar (from HK$550) is perfect for couples who enjoy nightlife with city views.

Budget Travel — Liuhe Night Market Base

Kaohsiung is one of Taiwan's most budget-friendly major cities. Stay near Liuhe Night Market in Xinxing District for the most affordable and convenient experience. Kindness Hotel Liuhe starts from just HK$280 per night and is steps from the night market, where you can fill up on seafood, papaya milk, and steak for a fraction of restaurant prices. Legend Hotel (from HK$250) near Central Park MRT offers strong value. Budget tips: travel during weekdays for 15-25% lower rates; book 2-3 weeks ahead for early-bird discounts; March-May and September-November are Kaohsiung's relative off-season with room rates 20-30% lower than peak periods.

Kenting Road Trip — Kaohsiung as Your Starting Point

Many travellers use Kaohsiung as a base for day trips or multi-day excursions to Kenting National Park, Taiwan's southern beach paradise, approximately 2 hours by car or express bus. Stay one or two nights in Kaohsiung to explore the city, then head south for beaches, snorkelling, and tropical scenery. Hotels near Zuoying HSR Station or Kaohsiung Main Station offer the most convenient starting points for Kenting-bound buses. Consider renting a car from Kaohsiung Airport or HSR Station for maximum flexibility. Howard Plaza Hotel (from HK$650) and Just Sleep (from HK$400) both offer parking and easy highway access.

Kaohsiung Hotel Accommodation FAQ

Q: Which area in Kaohsiung is ideal for staying?
For modern luxury and harbour views, the Asia New Bay Area in Qianzhen District is the top choice. For arts and culture, Yancheng near Pier-2 is recommended. For night markets and budget options, Xinxing near Liuhe Night Market. For design hotels, Qianjin near Central Park. For HSR connections, Zuoying. For family theme parks, Dashu/E-Da World.
Q: How do I get from Hong Kong to Kaohsiung?
Direct flights from Hong Kong to Kaohsiung International Airport (KHH) take approximately 1 hour 40 minutes. From the airport, take the MRT Red Line directly to the city centre (approximately 20 minutes to Formosa Boulevard Station, NT$35). Alternatively, taxis from the airport to central Kaohsiung cost approximately NT$300-400 (HK$75-100).
Q: How much does a Kaohsiung hotel cost per night?
Kaohsiung hotel price ranges: budget hotels HK$250-400/night, mid-range 4-star HK$400-700/night, five-star international brands HK$800-1,200/night. Prices rise 20-40% during Lunar New Year and summer holidays. Off-season (March-May, September-November) five-star hotels can be 20-30% less.
Q: Is Kaohsiung MRT transport convenient?
Kaohsiung has two MRT lines: the Red Line (north-south, connecting HSR Zuoying to Kaohsiung International Airport) and the Orange Line (east-west). The two lines intersect at Formosa Boulevard Station. A light rail circular line connects the Asia New Bay Area, Pier-2, and Hamasen. Fares range from NT$20-55 (approx. HK$5-14). Operating hours are approximately 06:00-24:00.
Q: What are the top attractions in Kaohsiung?
Top Kaohsiung attractions include: Pier-2 Art District, Sizihwan sunset views, Cijin Island (ferry from Gushan), Lotus Pond and Dragon Tiger Pagodas, Formosa Boulevard Dome of Light, Liuhe Night Market, Kaohsiung Music Centre, Fo Guang Shan Monastery, and E-Da World theme park. Most are accessible by MRT or light rail.
Q: Can I take a day trip to Kenting from Kaohsiung?
Yes, Kenting National Park is approximately 2 hours south by car or express bus from Kaohsiung. Kenting Express buses depart from Zuoying HSR Station (approximately NT$370 one way). Car rental is available at Kaohsiung Airport and HSR Station. A day trip is feasible but staying overnight in Kenting is recommended to fully enjoy the beaches and scenery.
Q: What is the weather like in Kaohsiung?
Kaohsiung has a tropical climate with warm weather year-round. Summer (June-September) is hot and humid with temperatures reaching 32-35 degrees Celsius, with occasional typhoons. Winter (December-February) is mild at 18-25 degrees. The most pleasant months for travel are October-April. Kaohsiung receives less rain than Taipei, making it a reliable destination even during Taiwan's rainy season.
Q: What food is Kaohsiung famous for?
Kaohsiung is renowned for seafood (especially at Cijin and Qijin harbours), papaya milk, salt-grilled squid, dan zai noodles, and Liuhe Night Market specialities including seafood congee and steak on hot plates. The city is also known for affordable and generous portions compared to Taipei. Grand Hi-Lai Hotel buffet is widely regarded as one of southern Taiwan's top hotel dining experiences.
Q: How do I get from Taipei to Kaohsiung?
The fastest option is Taiwan High Speed Rail (HSR), taking approximately 1.5 hours from Taipei to Zuoying Station. Standard tickets cost NT$1,490 (approx. HK$375). Early bird tickets offer up to 35% discount. Alternatively, domestic flights take approximately 1 hour. Conventional train (TRA) takes 4-5 hours but costs less. HSR is the most popular choice for its speed and convenience.
Q: Are there hotels near Liuhe Night Market?
Yes, several hotels are within walking distance of Liuhe Night Market in Xinxing District. Kindness Hotel Liuhe is right next to the market from HK$280. Just Sleep Kaohsiung Station is a short walk away from HK$400. Grand Hi-Lai Hotel is also nearby in Qianjin District from HK$700. Formosa Boulevard MRT Station serves the area with connections across the city.
Q: Which Kaohsiung hotels have harbour views?
Hotels with harbour or sea views include: Silks Club (Asia New Bay Area harbour views), Kaohsiung Marriott (harbour-facing rooms), InterContinental Kaohsiung (panoramic harbour views from rooftop bar), Chateau de Chine (selected harbour-view rooms), and 85 Sky Tower Hotel (high-floor panoramic views). The Asia New Bay Area offers the most concentrated harbour-view hotel options.
