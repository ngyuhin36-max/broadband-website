Shanghai Hotel Area Guide

The Bund / Huangpu District — Historic Architecture Showcase

The Bund is one of Shanghai's highly popular accommodation areas for tourists. The iconic row of heritage buildings along the riverfront is a signature Shanghai landmark. The area is home to top-tier international hotels including the Peace Hotel, Waldorf Astoria, and The Peninsula, with most rooms offering views of the Huangpu River and Pudong skyline. Nanjing East Road Pedestrian Street is within walking distance, and the night views are truly spectacular. 5-star hotels start from HK$900 per night — a top-rated area for first-time visitors to Shanghai.

Lujiazui / Pudong New Area — Skyscraper Skyline

Lujiazui is Shanghai's financial core, home to landmarks including the Oriental Pearl Tower, Shanghai World Financial Center, and Shanghai Tower. Top-tier hotels such as The Ritz-Carlton, Four Seasons, and Mandarin Oriental are concentrated here, with upper-floor rooms offering panoramic Bund views. Metro Line 2 Lujiazui Station provides excellent transport links — a top choice for business travellers and those seeking high-altitude views. 5-star hotels from HK$1,600.

Nanjing Road / People's Square — Shopping & Transport Hub

Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street is Shanghai's shopping core, and People's Square is a major transport hub where Metro Lines 1, 2, and 8 converge. The area offers diverse hotel options from budget to 5-star. The Bund is within walking distance, and the Shanghai Museum and Shanghai Grand Theatre are nearby. Ideal for first-time visitors who prioritise transport convenience. 4-star hotels from HK$450.

Jing'an Temple / Jing'an District — Upscale Business & Living

Jing'an District is Shanghai's upscale business and lifestyle area, with West Nanjing Road hosting major international luxury brands and premium malls. Top-rated hotels including Shangri-La and The PuLi are located here, offering an elegant atmosphere that is peaceful despite the central location. Jing'an Temple has rich historical heritage, and the area also offers diverse dining and cultural experiences. Ideal for travellers seeking premium lifestyle experiences. 5-star hotels from HK$1,200.

Xintiandi / Huaihai Road — Fashionable Cultural Landmark

Xintiandi is Shanghai's fashionable cultural landmark, featuring restaurants, bars, and boutiques housed in restored Shikumen lane-house architecture. The Andaz hotel is centrally located, within walking distance of diverse international dining. Huaihai Road is Shanghai's trendy shopping street, ideal for travellers seeking cultural experiences and nightlife. 5-star hotels from HK$1,400.

Disneyland / Pudong — Family Holiday Destination

Shanghai Disney Resort is located in Pudong New Area and is mainland China's first Disney theme park. The Disneyland Hotel and Toy Story Hotel are the official resort hotels, with guests enjoying early park entry privileges. There are also several budget hotel options nearby. Ideal for family trips — we recommend planning a stay of 2 days/1 night or more. Disney hotels from HK$1,500, nearby budget hotels from HK$350.

Accommodation Picks by Traveller Type

Family Travel — Disneyland Top Pick

For families visiting Shanghai with children, Shanghai Disneyland Hotel is the top pick, with early park entry privileges, themed rooms and children's pools — an excellent choice for family trips. For budget-conscious families, consider nearby economy hotels with free shuttle buses to the park. In the city centre, the Hyatt on the Bund offers pools and family facilities within walking distance of The Bund and Nanjing Road. We recommend choosing a breakfast-inclusive package, as Shanghai hotel buffet breakfasts offer extensive options — the whole family can fuel up before heading out. Recommended family itinerary: Disneyland + The Bund + Nanjing Road + Shanghai Science & Technology Museum + Wild Animal Park.

Couples Getaway — Romantic Bund Night Views

For couples visiting Shanghai, Bund hotels are the top choice. The Peace Hotel's historic charm paired with its rooftop jazz bar is a classic highlight. The W Hotel's rooftop pool bar with Pudong skyline views is a highly popular choice for young couples. For a romantic, artistic atmosphere, choose the Andaz Xintiandi — Shikumen culture meets modern design with exceptional style. For anniversaries or proposals, The Peninsula Shanghai is recommended for its exceptionally attentive service and top-rated guest scores. Business Travel — Lujiazui & Jing'an First

Business travellers highly value transport links and meeting facilities. Lujiazui hosts The Ritz-Carlton, Four Seasons, and Mandarin Oriental, all adjacent to major financial institutions and international conference centres. Jing'an District's Shangri-La is at the heart of the West Nanjing Road business district with comprehensive business facilities. Most 5-star hotels offer executive lounge access, including complimentary afternoon tea and happy hour. For Pudong Airport access, we recommend staying in Lujiazui with direct Metro Line 2 connection; for Hongqiao Airport, the Jing'an area is more convenient.

Backpackers / Budget Travel — Nanjing Road & Jing'an Savings Tips

For budget Shanghai hotels, Home Inn Plus and Ji Hotel are top choices from HK$300 per night. Nanjing Road and Jing'an District offer many affordable options with convenient transport. Booking tips: avoid the National Day Golden Week (October 1-7) and Chinese New Year, book 2-3 weeks in advance for early-bird deals, and take advantage of platform member discounts. Additionally, December to February is Shanghai's off-peak season (cold and damp winter), with room rates as low as 50-60% of peak season prices. There are also several youth hostels and guesthouses near The Bund, perfect for backpackers wanting to experience the Bund night views.

Shanghai Hotel FAQs

Q: Which area in Shanghai is recommended for hotels?
Sightseeing travellers should consider The Bund/Nanjing Road first, with walking access to heritage architecture and Huangpu River night views. Business travellers are recommended Lujiazui or Jing'an District. For culture and nightlife, choose Xintiandi. Family trips suit Disneyland Resort or Hyatt on the Bund.
Q: What hotels are recommended near The Bund?
Top-rated Bund hotels include: Fairmont Peace Hotel (20 Nanjing East Road), Waldorf Astoria (2 Zhongshan East 1st Road), and The Peninsula (32 Zhongshan East 1st Road). The Hyatt on the Bund on the northern stretch offers excellent value from HK$900 with Bund views.
Q: Where to stay for Shanghai Disneyland?
The top pick is Shanghai Disneyland Hotel (from HK$1,500), with early park entry privileges for guests. For tighter budgets, nearby economy hotels start from HK$250 with free shuttle buses to the park. From the city centre, Metro Line 11 takes about 50 minutes to Disneyland Station.
Q: How much does a Shanghai hotel cost per night?
Shanghai hotel prices range widely: budget (Home Inn/Ji Hotel) HK$250-450/night, mid-range 4-star HK$450-800/night, 5-star international brands HK$900-2,800/night. Peak season (National Day, Labour Day) prices increase 30-50%. Off-season winter 5-star hotels can start from HK$700.
Q: Where to stay in Shanghai during winter?
Winter temperatures in Shanghai range from 0-10°C, mainly cold and damp. We recommend hotels with good heating — most reputable hotels have central heating or air conditioning heating. Consider staying on Nanjing Road or in Jing'an District to minimise outdoor walking time. Winter is the off-season with very attractive 5-star hotel prices from HK$700-1,000 for international brands.
Q: Where to stay in Shanghai during summer?
Shanghai summers are hot and humid (June-August averages 30-38°C). We recommend choosing hotels with pools to cool off. The W Hotel's rooftop pool, Hyatt on the Bund's outdoor pool, and The Ritz-Carlton's indoor pool are all highly popular. Summer is peak season — book 2-3 weeks in advance.
Q: What is breakfast like at Shanghai hotels?
5-star hotel buffet breakfasts typically offer Chinese, Western, and Japanese options with excellent quality, at CNY 150-280/person (approx. HK$165-308). The Peace Hotel and Peninsula Hotel breakfast experiences are highly recommended. Mid-range hotel breakfasts cost CNY 50-80/person with simpler but sufficient options. For authentic Shanghai breakfast, find a local shop for pan-fried buns or xiaolongbao near the hotel. We recommend booking breakfast-inclusive packages — usually 30% cheaper than buying separately.
Q: What should travellers know about staying in Shanghai?
Important reminders: (1) Check-in requires a valid passport or travel document; (2) Some budget hotels may not accept foreign guests — we recommend choosing international brands or hotels marked as accepting foreign guests on Trip.com; (3) Shanghai hotels require real-name registration; (4) Download WeChat Pay/Alipay in advance for daily purchases; (5) Shanghai internet has firewall restrictions — a VPN is needed for Google/WhatsApp etc.
Q: Is Shanghai's metro transport convenient?
Shanghai's metro network is very well-developed, with over 19 lines covering all major areas. Fares are CNY 3-9 (approx. HK$3.3-10), much cheaper than taxis. We recommend staying near a metro station (within a 5-minute walk). Line 2 (Nanjing Road/Lujiazui/Hongqiao/Pudong Airport) and Line 10 (Nanjing Road/Xintiandi/Hongqiao) are the most useful for travellers. Rush hours are very crowded — avoid 7:30-9:30 and 17:00-19:00 if possible.
Q: Which Shanghai hotels have swimming pools?
Shanghai hotels with pools include: W Shanghai (rooftop infinity pool), Hyatt on the Bund (outdoor pool with Bund views), The Ritz-Carlton (indoor heated pool), and Shanghai Disneyland Hotel (indoor and outdoor pools). Some hotels open outdoor pools in summer, while winter swimming is in indoor heated pools.
Q: How to get to Shanghai from Hong Kong?
Main options from Hong Kong to Shanghai: (1) Direct flights take approximately 2.5 hours, landing at Pudong Airport or Hongqiao Airport; (2) High-speed rail from West Kowloon Station takes about 8 hours direct to Shanghai Hongqiao Station. Flights are faster but factoring in airport transit time the difference narrows. From Pudong Airport, the Maglev train takes about 8 minutes to Longyang Road Station, then transfer to metro; from Hongqiao Airport, Metro Line 2 goes directly to the city centre. We recommend staying near Nanjing Road/Jing'an for convenient airport transport.
Q: What time is recommended for viewing The Bund night lights?
The Bund light show runs nightly — summer (May-October) lighting hours are approximately 18:30-22:00, winter (November-April) approximately 17:30-21:00. We recommend arriving 30 minutes before sunset to enjoy the gradual transition from dusk to night. Staying at a Bund hotel (Peace Hotel, Waldorf Astoria, etc.) allows you to enjoy the views from your room. Weekends and holidays draw larger crowds — allow extra time.
