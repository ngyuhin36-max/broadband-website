How to Find Cheap Flights

Tue/Wed Typically Cheapest

Airlines typically release promotions on Tue/Wed, with fares 20-30% cheaper than Fri-Sun departures.

Book 1-3 Months Early

Short-haul: book 4-6 weeks ahead. Long-haul: 2-3 months. This usually gets the most competitive prices.

Use Price Comparison

Trip.com offers price trends and prediction tools. Set alerts to get notified when prices drop.

Red-eye Flights Cheaper

Early morning flights are usually cheaper. Arrive and start your trip right away.

Connecting Flights Save More

Direct flights are convenient but pricier. One connection can save up to 40%. Allow at least 2hr layover.

Budget vs Full-Service

HK Express, Peach offer lower fares but charge for baggage. Compare total cost including luggage.

Flight FAQs

Q: What's the cheapest flight from Hong Kong to Tokyo?
Hong Kong to Tokyo return economy from HK$1,280 incl. tax. HK Express sales can go as low as HK$980. Book 4-6 weeks early, avoid Golden Week and summer peak. Tue/Wed departures are typically cheapest.
Q: HK Express vs Cathay Pacific — which is cheaper?
HK Express has lower base fares but excludes baggage and meals. Adding 20kg checked bag (HK$200-300) may close the gap with Cathay. Choose HK Express for 1-2 day trips, Cathay for longer trips with more luggage.
Q: When is the cheapest time to buy flights?
Short-haul (Japan/Korea/Thailand): 4-6 weeks before departure. Long-haul (Europe/US): 2-3 months ahead. January, May and September are major airline sale seasons. Tuesday and Wednesday afternoons often have flash deals.
Q: How long is the flight from Hong Kong to Bangkok?
Direct flight ~2.5 hours, one of the most popular short-haul destinations for HK travellers. Return from HK$880 incl. tax. Thai Lion Air and HK Express often have promos as low as HK$600.
Q: Can I cancel flights for free?
Depends on fare type. Full-service flexible fares usually allow free changes/cancellation. Budget basic fares are non-refundable — add "flexible rebooking" option (~HK$100-200). Some Trip.com fares offer "Free Cancellation".
Q: Cheapest airline from Hong Kong to Seoul?
HK Express and Air Busan offer the lowest prices, return from HK$1,180. Cathay and Korean Air with baggage/meals from HK$2,000. During promotions HK Express can go as low as HK$800.
Q: How much are budget airline baggage fees?
HK Express checked 20kg: HK$200-350 (varies by route), carry-on 7kg free. Peach baggage: HK$180-280. Add baggage at booking — airport counter is 50%+ more expensive.
Q: Tips for flying with children from Hong Kong?
Infants under 2 typically pay 10% of adult fare with no separate seat. Children 2-11 pay 75-90% on most airlines. Pre-book a bassinet seat for babies. Choose full-service airlines for included kids' meals and baggage. Baby formula and food are exempt from liquid restrictions at security.
Q: When should I book Japan cherry blossom flights?
Cherry blossom season is typically late March to mid-April. Book by late January to early February for the most competitive prices — fares rise 30-50% during peak season. HK Express sales may still offer Tokyo returns under HK$1,500. Osaka and Kyoto are equally popular during this period.
Q: Are connecting flights worth it? Are they safe?
Connecting flights are perfectly safe — major airlines arrange through-check baggage. You can save 20-40% by connecting, e.g., London via Dubai saves HK$800+. Allow at least 2 hours for layovers and choose same-airline or alliance flights for better protection. If time isn't an issue, connecting flights are a great money saver.
Q: How to find the cheapest flights from Hong Kong?
5 money-saving tips: 1) Use flexible date search — shifting 1-2 days can save hundreds. 2) Fly Tuesday-Wednesday for typically cheaper fares. 3) Subscribe to airline sale alerts. 4) Book 4-6 weeks early for short-haul, 2-3 months for long-haul. 5) Use Trip.com to compare all airlines in one search.
Q: How much are flights to Vietnam? Do I need a visa?
Hong Kong to Ho Chi Minh City return from HK$980, ~2.5 hours. Cathay Pacific, Vietnam Airlines and HK Express all fly direct. HKSAR passport and BNO holders need an e-Visa (~US$25, 3 business days). Hanoi flights are similarly priced.

Hong Kong Flight Route Guide

Japan — Hong Kong's Favourite Destination

Japan is a top destination for Hong Kong travellers. With the weak yen, travel offers exceptional value. Tokyo return from HK$1,280, Osaka HK$1,180, Okinawa HK$980. Cathay Pacific and HK Express are among the most popular airlines. Both Narita and Haneda airports have direct flights, ~4 hours. Avoid Golden Week (late Apr-early May) and Obon (mid-Aug) when prices jump 50%+. Cherry blossom season (late Mar-mid Apr) and autumn foliage (Nov) are ideal but book 2 months ahead.

South Korea — K-pop & Food Paradise

Seoul return from HK$1,380, another highly popular destination. Incheon Airport has convenient airport express to Seoul Station. HK Express, Korean Air and Asiana all fly direct. Busan is rising in popularity, return from HK$1,180 — Haeundae Beach and Gamcheon Culture Village are Instagram hotspots. Korea offers 90-day visa-free entry. Avoid Chuseok and Lunar New Year when prices surge.

Southeast Asia — Great Budget Short-Haul

Bangkok return from HK$880 is one of the cheapest short-haul destinations. Singapore HK$1,080, Vietnam HK$980, Bali HK$1,680. Rich airline choice with both full-service and budget carriers. Thailand offers 30-day visa-free. Vietnam requires e-visa. Avoid rainy season (Jun-Oct); Nov-Feb has very comfortable weather.

Taiwan — Shortest & Most Convenient

Taipei return from HK$780 with just a 1.5-hour flight — one of the shortest international routes. Cathay, China Airlines, EVA Air, HK Express all have multiple daily flights. Taoyuan Airport Express goes direct to Taipei Main Station. Night markets, Jiufen, Taipei 101 are must-visits. 30-day visa-free entry.

Europe & Americas — Book Early, Save Big

London return from HK$4,280, Paris HK$4,580, New York HK$5,880. Long-haul prices fluctuate significantly — booking 2-3 months early can save up to 30%. Cathay Pacific offers direct HK-London, New York, Paris flights (12-16 hours). Connecting flights via Dubai or Singapore are usually cheaper but take longer. Book fares including baggage for long trips.

Route-Specific Price FAQs

Q: What are the cheapest Hong Kong to Osaka flights?
Hong Kong to Osaka (Kansai Airport) return from HK$1,180 incl. tax. HK Express and Peach Aviation are among the cheapest, sales as low as HK$880. Cathay Pacific with baggage/meals from HK$1,800. ~3.5hr flight, book 4-6 weeks early.
Q: Are there direct flights from Hong Kong to Okinawa?
Yes! HK Express and Peach Aviation offer direct flights to Okinawa (Naha Airport), ~2.5 hours. Return from HK$980 incl. tax. Okinawa is Japan's closest resort — perfect for 3-4 day beach trips.
Q: Best airline from Hong Kong to Singapore?
Singapore Airlines has top-rated service but costs more (from HK$1,800). Cathay Pacific offers good value (from HK$1,400). Scoot is the budget option (from HK$1,080 excl. baggage). ~3.5hr flight.
Q: When are Hong Kong to Taipei flights cheapest?
HK to Taipei is the shortest international route (1.5hr), return from HK$780. Weekdays (Tue-Thu) are 30% cheaper. Avoid CNY, Dragon Boat, Mid-Autumn. Late Apr-May and Oct-Nov are ideal travel seasons.
Q: Cathay Pacific vs HK Express to Japan — which is better?
Cathay includes 23kg baggage + meals + seat selection, Tokyo return from HK$2,200. HK Express base fare HK$1,280 plus baggage (HK$200-350). With 20kg luggage, the gap is only HK$300-500 — Cathay offers much better value. Light 2-3 day trips: HK Express. Normal trips: Cathay.
Q: Cheapest Hong Kong to London flights? How long?
Cathay Pacific direct to Heathrow ~12hr, return from HK$4,280. Via Dubai (Emirates) from HK$3,500 but 18-20hr total. British Airways direct from HK$4,500. Book 2-3 months early. Summer and Christmas are peak pricing.

January, May and September are major airline sale months.

Business travellers prioritise direct flights with good timing and flexible rebooking. Cathay Pacific business class is reliable across Asia, with return fares from HK$5,000-12,000. Seasonal Flight Guide from Hong Kong

Spring (Mar-May) — Cherry Blossom Season

Japan's cherry blossom season (late March to mid-April) is the year's hottest travel period, with fares rising 30-50%. Book at least 2 months ahead. Korea's cherry blossoms are 1-2 weeks later — Yeouido in Seoul and Jinhae are top spots. In Southeast Asia, Bangkok flights spike around Songkran (April 13-15).

Summer (Jun-Aug) — Peak Season

Summer holidays are typically the most expensive time to fly, especially mid-July to late August. Departing in early June or early September saves 30%+. Okinawa and Bali are top summer beach destinations. Long-haul fares to Europe/US jump 50%+ — if you can avoid peak summer, savings are significant.

Autumn (Sep-Nov) — Great Deals + Autumn Foliage

September is a major airline sale month — one of the cheapest times to buy flights all year. Japan's autumn foliage season (November) makes Kyoto and Tokyo hugely popular. Seoul's autumn colours and ginkgo trees are stunning. Thailand enters cool season from November — very comfortable travel weather.

Winter (Dec-Feb) — Christmas & Lunar New Year

Christmas and Lunar New Year are the second-most expensive periods. Depart early December to avoid Christmas peak. Mid-January to early February (around CNY) sees Japan/Korea/Thailand fares surge 50-80%. If you don't mind the cold, January off-peak to Europe (London, Paris) can be as low as HK$3,500 return. Hokkaido ski season (Dec-Mar) has high demand — book early.
